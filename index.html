Overview

The siege of Corinth was started on April 29th, 1862. It ended on April 30th, 1862. Generel Henry Halleck captured the town of Corinth, Mississippi, which had 2 railroads. This was a Union Victory because General P. G. T. Beauregard realized that Henry Halleck's army was really close to Corinth. Because of their disease and lack of water, they surrendered the town without having a big fight. Click on the navigation icon to see more.

Timeline

Before the battle

After the many Confederate defeats, General P. G. T. Beauregard sent his army to Corinth and built up the defences and got reinforcements. General Henry W. Halleck got over 100,000 troops from 3 armies at Pittsburg Landing to prepare to go to Corinth.

4/29/1862

Henry Halleck sent over 100,000 men from 3 armies to prepare to move from Pittsburg Landing to Corinth. Halleck moved his army in a very cautious manner in 3 wings to attempt to prevent another surprise attack like the Battle of Shiloh. George H. Thomas commanded the right wing which was the Army of Tennessee. Don Carlos Buell commanded the middle wing which was the Army of Ohio. John Pope commanded the left wing which was the Army of the Mississippi.

5/3/1862

Henry Halleck's army got close to Corinth. The Union and Confederate had lots of combat at Farmington, the Russel House, and the Widow Surratt Farm. Every day, the Union slowly approached Corinth.

5/28/1862

Henry Halleck did an attack using his entire force. He ordered George H. Thomas to attack a double log house, which was <1 mi. from the Confederate positions. Don Carlos Buell planned to take control of the Surratt Farm and the hill which overlooked it. After a short fight, Don Carlos Buell took the hill. John pope fought near the Shelton house to his left and maintained their position. Halleck became close enough to bombard the city.

Aftermath

On May 29th, 1862, P. G. T. Beauregard evacuated Corinth by train. To fool the Union, the Confederate army was told to cheer when a train came to trick the Union into thinking that they were recieving reinforcements. On the morning of May 30th, 1862, the Union army found that the Confederate troops left Corinth.
